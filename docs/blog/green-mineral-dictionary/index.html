The mineral suppply chain is a major geopolitical issue. I’m particularly interested in the sensitive ones needed for batteries and motors (turns out solar mostly just needs copper and aluminum), so I created this overview.

Contents: Battery Minerals | Motor Minerals | Sources

Note:

  • This post omits coal, iron, and aluminum.

Battery Minerals

  • Lithium – Abundant but concentrated
    • Used in all commercial EV battery cathodes.
    • Mined in: China, Chile, Argentina, Australia, Zimbabwe
    • Refined in: TODO
  • Nickel – Abundant (battery-grade rarer)
    • Used in high energy-density cathodes like NCM (Nickel-Cobalt-Manganese) and NCA (Nickel-Cobalt-Aluminum). Higher nickel content increases EV range.
    • Mined in: Indonesia, Phillippines, New Caledonia, Australia, China, Russia, Canada
  • Manganese – Moderately abundant –
    • Used in NCM and LMO (Lithium-Manganese Oxide) cathodes. Offers thermal stability and affordability.
    • Mined in: South Africa, Gabon, Australia, Ghana, China, India, Brazil
  • Graphite – Abundant
    • Used in nearly all Li-ion battery anodes. Natural or synthetic. High purity is required.
    • Mined in: China, mostly
  • Copper – Abundant
    • Key in battery busbars, interconnects, and current collectors (usually anode side).
    • Mined in: Chile, Peru, DRC, China, United States, Russia, and more
    • Refined in: China mostly, also Chile, DRC, Japan, Russia, and United States
  • Cerium – Abundant
    • Utilized in some battery technologies and as a catalyst in certain chemical processes.
  • Lanthanum – Rare
    • Used in nickel-metal hydride (NiMH) batteries, which are often used in hybrid vehicles for their stability and energy density.
  • Yttrium – Rare
    • Sometimes used in conjunction with other materials to improve battery performance and longevity.
  • Cobalt – Rare and expensive
    • Stabilizes layered cathode structures in NCM/NCA chemistries. Efforts exist to reduce or eliminate it due to ethical and supply issues.
    • Mined in: DRC mostly, some from Indonesia
    • Refined in: China, mostly, some from Finland

Motor Minerals

Most high-performance EVs use Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motors (PMSMs) due to their compact size, high torque density, and efficiency. These motors require NdFeB (Neodymium-Iron-Boron) magnets enhanced with other REEs:

  • Copper – Abundant
    • Essential for windings in electric motors due to its excellent conductivity.
  • Silicon – Abundant
    • Often added to iron to enhance magnetic properties in electrical steel used in motor cores.
    • Mined in: China mostly, some from Russia, Brazil, Norway, and US
  • Neodymium (Nd) – Rare
    • Core magnetic material in NdFeB magnets. Critical for powerful, compact motors with high torque. *Refined in:
  • Praseodymium (Pr) – Rare
    • Often combined with neodymium (Nd-Pr alloys) to slightly improve magnetic performance and reduce cost.
  • Dysprosium (Dy) – Very rare
    • Added to improve thermal stability, so magnets retain magnetism under high motor temperatures.
  • Terbium (Tb) – Very rare
    • Used as an additive in magnets to improve their performance.
  • Boron – Rare
    • Used in the production of high-performance magnets and to enhance the magnetic properties of materials.
