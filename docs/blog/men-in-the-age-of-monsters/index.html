GREAT MEN IN AN AGE OF MONSTERS

The Epstein files have made it clear that the United States is going through an age of monsters, where the rich and powerful can be as evil as they want and face absolutely zero consequences. This seems to have been the case for quite some time, but it has never been this apparent, this obvious, this in-you-face.

At the same time, we’re undergoing a “male loneliness epidemic”, where so many men lack a future, purpose, and basic social skills that many of the man-hating feminists from the past decade now pity them.

This is bad.

In an era of desparate men, you need growth, honor, and hard-work to be the obvious path to success. Not crime depravity, and corruption. I’m afraid of what our neighbors will become when all their north stars are monsters.

It’s unfortunate, because most of us—young men especially—want great people to look up to. That’s why Superman and Captain America are so popular, why George Washington and Abraham Lincoln have their legacies, and why guys like samurais. There’s absolutely a hunger for great men (and great women too, obviously, just let me use the term).

There are some good male influencers out there, but none that seem to dominate the narrative. Old-school Jordan Peterson filled a void for a lot of lost men before he became popular, and he didn’t even do this by example; he just played what was essentially just an emotionally intelligent father who told his followers to get their shit together. There’s the Old Creek Rangler school guy. But I don’t really know anyone in the mainstream who fits the character of a “great man” now—it’s mostly monsters (or people who support monsters). And for each “good” male influencer there’s an equivalent monster (e.g, the “man-o-sphere”).

There appears to be two ways out of this:

  1. Voting these people into jail, or
  2. A cultural shift that forces the monsters back into the shadows.

The monsters will try to rig the election, so the only solution in our hands is cultural change.

Such a change would require some “great men” to establish an unignorable social media/hollywood prescence. Without the help of a monstrous establishment, this kind of prescence requires rage/click-bait, advertising and so on to game the algorithms and news cycle, all of which pepper the path of corruption. The people who have what it takes to become a household name today don’t seem to make it out clean (except for Mamdani, who seems clean but hasn’t proven himself yet).
